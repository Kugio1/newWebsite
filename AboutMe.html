  • Bromley FC

Bromley Football Club is a professional association football club based in Bromley, Greater London, England. They are currently members of the National League, the fifth level of the English football league system. They will play in EFL League Two from the 2024–25 season after gaining promotion through the play-offs. Bromley play their home matches at Hayes Lane.

  • Bromley Rugby Club

Bromley Rugby Football Club was founded in 1886 as Catford Bridge Football Club and was originally associated with St George’s Church in Catford. The Club quickly gained early recognition and respect, notwithstanding a somewhat nomadic existence. The original club colours were terracotta jerseys with purple collars, but in 1895 the club adopted the black and amber colours of Middlesex Wanderers, who had disbanded. They also have a girls team.

  • Biggin Hill Airport

London Biggin Hill Airport generated £200 million to the UK economy in 2023 and has the potential to deliver more than £630 million in the future. With indirect and induced supply chain effects considered, this translates to £142.9 million (72%) captured within the London Borough of Bromley, directly benefitting local residents, and £154 million within London. Biggin Hill was firmly established as a key experimental base for new technologies during the inter-war period such as sound location devices developed between 1919 and 1936 for the detection of approaching aircraft, hence the airport.

  • Bromley Parks

Bromley is undoubtedly London’s greenest borough, encompassing over 160 parks, 52 allotments and 21 outdoor sports facilities. The Council is rightly proud of its custodianship of 7000 acres of green space on behalf of Bromley’s 300,000 residents. Much of the countryside around Charles Darwin’s former home at Downe is owned by Bromley Council and has unique cultural significance as the ‘open-air laboratory’ in which Darwin developed his theory of evolution by natural selection. Bromley council is home to many parks; one of the biggest parks in south London is in Bromley - Crystal Palace Park. Crystal Palace Park is a large park in south-east London, Grade II* listed on the Register of Historic Parks and Gardens. It was laid out in the 1850s as a pleasure ground, centred around the re-location of The Crystal Palace - the largest glass building of the time - from central London to this area on the border of Kent and Surrey; the suburb that grew around the park is known by the same name.

Bromley Parks Official Website

  • The Glades - Bromley Shopping Centre

The Glades is a shopping centre in Bromley, London. It has a total of 135 stores of every kind you can think of (shoe stores, fast food, jewellery stores etc...). The name "The Glades" was chosen following a competition in which Bromley residents were asked for suggestions. "The Glades" was chosen reflecting the history of Bromley as a wooded area. The centre was rebranded as Intu Bromley in the summer of 2013 following the renaming of parent Capital Shopping Centres Group plc as Intu Properties plc.

  • Ambulance Service in Bromley

The ambulance service in Bromley has a main role, to respond to emergency 999 calls, providing medical care to patients across the capital, and to be prepared for dealing with large-scale or major incidents in the capital 24-hours a day, 365 days a year. Other services they offer include providing pre-arranged patient transport and finding hospital beds, working with the police and the fire service. There are many ambulance stations in Bromley council, to name some: Beckenham Ambulance Station, Mottingham Ambulance Station, St. Paul's Cray Ambulance Station.

  • Police Stations in Bromley

Bromley police station serves as the territorial police force responsible for law enforcement and crime prevention within the ceremonial county of Greater London/South East London. In addition, it is responsible for specialised tasks throughout the city, such as UK counter-terrorism measures, and the protection of certain high-profile individuals. There's only one other police station in Bromley, and that's Orpington Police Station.
